About the Program

West Side Basketball develops competitive youth players through high-level coaching, structured practices, and AAU tournament play.

Focused training on fundamentals — ball handling, shooting mechanics, defensive footwork, and basketball IQ. Every player gets better.

Competitive Play

Next-Level Prep

Preparing players for high school varsity and beyond. We build the habits, discipline, and confidence that translate to the next stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What age groups do you offer?

We offer teams for youth and middle school players. Specific age divisions will be determined based on registration numbers and tryout results.

Does my child need prior AAU experience?

No. Tryouts are open to all skill levels. We evaluate effort, coachability, and potential — not just current ability.

What is the time commitment?

Teams typically practice 2–3 times per week with tournaments on weekends during the AAU season. A full schedule is provided after team placement.

What does the tryout fee cover?

The tryout fee covers the evaluation session. Season fees (covering coaching, gym time, tournament entry, and uniforms) are separate and communicated after team placement.

What if my child doesn't make a team?

We aim to place every player who tries out. If we can't offer a spot, we'll provide feedback and recommendations for development.

Who are the coaches?

Our program is led by Marcus Draney, an experienced coach with a passion for player development. Learn more about our coaching staff.
